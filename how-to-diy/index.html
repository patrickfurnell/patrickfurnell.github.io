Tips on How to Remodel a Bathroom

Connect with a bathroom remodeling specialist

 
There are many reasons to remodel your bathroom. Perhaps it has old or outdated fixtures, or it has suffered structural damage over the years. Maybe it’s too small or cramped. It may not have adequate lighting or storage space, or maybe you are simply bored with it.

Cosmetics or Wrecking Balls?

If a simple paint job or tub resurfacing will solve the problem, your makeover may not cost much or require much advanced planning. If it requires major changes that involve construction or fixture removal, a little more strategy is needed.

How to Remodel a Bathroom: Professional or DIY?

Remodeling a room is a major project, but carefully planning and prioritizing makes it easier. You can save money by doing simpler work yourself. This might include tiling, painting or installing new wall accessories. If you have the necessary skills and experience, you can attempt electrical, plumbing or construction work. If not, it is best to hire a professional.

Planning the Bathroom and the Budget

Before you start, make a list of everything you would like to change, and start getting estimates of how much it will cost to implement your ideas. Be sure that you include everything in your budget and leave some wiggle room for unexpected expenses.

Start With a Checklist

Before proceeding with a remodel, it can be very helpful to have a bathroom remodeling checklist on hand for reference. These step by step instructions can be a great way to keep your project on track and on time. Here is a basic step-by-step guide to get you started on the remodel design:

1. Start with a Plan

Before beginning any remodel, homeowners should draw up a detailed sketch of the new space that includes the exact placements and measurements of all fixtures. This will ensure that homeowners purchase the right size of fixtures such as toilets and sinks and the right amount of materials such as trim or tiles. Homeowners can find a wealth of inspiration from magazines, home improvement stores and the many home improvement and home design blogs and websites that are available on the internet.

2. Gather Materials

Once homeowners know what they would like their new bathrooms to look like, the next step is to gather together all of the materials before they begin. This includes not only the major pieces such as the sink and toilet, but also all of the smaller items such as screws and washers as well. For a complete bathroom overhaul, homeowners will need materials such as dry wall, wall putty, a putty knife, tiles and grout. A homeowner may also choose to buy new towel racks, a new medicine cabinet or new shelves for storage as well.

3. Out with the Old

Once they have finished preparing, homeowners can begin their remodel by taking out all of the old furnishing and fixtures that they will be replacing. Homeowners should be careful when removing items, however, so that nothing gets destroyed that they were hoping to keep. Items that homeowners will not need anymore can be donated to used home improvement stores such as Habitat for Humanity’s ReStore.

4. In with the New

Once all of the old furnishings and fixtures have been removed, it is time to start adding in the new bathroom components. Homeowners should always start from the top of the bathroom and work their way down. This will help prevent the new floor from getting ruined when the walls are being painted. Homeowners can install the bathtub, toilet or sink first before installing the medicine cabinet and shelves. Then homeowners can paint, install the flooring and add the trim.

5. Clean Up

Remodeling can make a huge mess. Once all of the old fixtures have been replaced with new ones, homeowners need to make sure to clean the new bathroom thoroughly. Dust and paint flecks should be wiped off of surfaces, and floors should be cleaned. Fixture packaging will need to be thrown away, and paint brushes will need to be cleaned as well.

6. Decorate

Lastly, homeowners can have fun decorating their new bathrooms to fit their unique styles and tastes. Homeowners can choose paint colors and art pieces for the walls. They can choose new shower curtains and rugs. They can even install small shelves that can be used to display a variety of decorative or functional pieces such as jars of Q-tips, hand towels or decorative soaps.

Bathroom remodeling can be a stressful project as families have to learn to live with one less bathroom, but it can also be a very fun project as well. Choosing all new fixtures and furnishings is a great way for families to create a bathroom that reflects their unique personality and that is the perfect relaxing getaway inside their very own home.

Don’t Settle

There are thousands of possibilities when remodeling a bathroom, so if some ideas turn out to be too expensive, keep brainstorming until you come across a good compromise. Search for inspiration in home decorating magazines, online and in home improvement stores.

Follow the Right Steps When Remodeling Your Bathroom

One of the most important elements of remodeling a bathroom is doing the work in the right order. If this is not strictly followed, successful installations may have to be dismantled and re-done.

  • Update and repair electricity and plumbing.
  • Install and paint drywall.
  • Prior to tiling, install cabinets.
  • Tile walls before floors and accents.
  • Finally, place the toilet.

Maximizing the Small Bathroom

Professional decorators know how to remodel a bathroom using tips and tricks to alter the perception of space and dimension. It’s not always possible to extend the size of a room, but there are ways to give it a bright, spacious feel. Use large vanity mirrors to dramatically increase the sense of openness.

For poor lighting conditions, consider incorporating light-reflecting glass mosaic tile. Allow more light in by enlarging windows; instead of standard panes, consider block glass or frosted privacy glass. Use warm, light colors; these have the same room-opening effect as white, but they don’t show dirt as easily. Drawing the attention upward adds dimension to small rooms. You can accomplish this with accessories and accent walls.

Beyond the Look

Base your decisions on practical design elements as well as aesthetic appeal. Adding energy-efficient lighting, skylights, additional windows and water-conserving shower heads can make your home greener and save energy cost. Choose neutral colors for permanent features such as tile or countertops. Flashy or trendy looks will soon become dated and can make it difficult to resell the home.

Don’t Neglect Safety Issues

Be sure that your flooring choices are safe for your household. If you have elderly people living with you, they might easily fall on slippery glass tiles or non-textured shower floors. When safety, practicality, cost and beauty are all balanced, your new bathroom can give you both a luxurious retreat and a reason to be proud.
