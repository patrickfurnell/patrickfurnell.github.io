Remember, You Cannot Remodel Your Foundation

 
When you are building or buying your first home, it is important to pay special attention to your foundation. The foundation is an important part of your home because it holds everything up and protects your home from moisture, insects and other issues that can make your home unsafe. A cracked or leaking foundation can lead to mold growing in your home and to serious health problems.

A good construction crew will take the time to do your foundation correctly. Making a foundation out of hollow cinder blocks can lead to infestation problems in the future. If your builder is using this type of foundation, you may want to talk about alternatives. In addition to the type of foundation you use, whether you have a basement, crawl space or a slab, you should make sure that the concrete is poured correctly. Often construction will be halted until the conditions are correct for the foundation to be poured and to give the foundation time to cure properly before building begins.

When you are building your home, the plans usually specify the type of foundation that is used. You may want to talk to the builder about how well that works with the soil conditions in your area. You should also take into account any chance of flooding you may have and build an elevated foundation to avoid any flooding issues in the future. If you are buying an already built home, you should carefully inspect the foundation for cracks and leaking, even if the home is fairly new. Although, you can remodel and repair nearly any aspect of your home, foundation repair is very expensive and time consuming.
Your foundation is the key to your home staying safe and secure. As you prepare to build or look to buy a home, take the time to make sure the foundation is in good condition. Use a professional home inspector to examine it and the crawl space to keep your home and family safe.
